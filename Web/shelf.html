Everyone is Gone

Everyone is Gone is a short game in bisty, an engine defined by constraints what with its low pixel count, small word limit per text box, and a max of three-colors per room among other things. Because of the engine, I had to really focus on design details; making sure camera perspective were coherent to players—top down or from the side—and the item descriptions—as all text in this game is internal dialogue—were written in such a way that players understood the overall story was a challenge, but it was the first time I really felt like a Narrative Designer. Coming up with the story, figuring out how that could be portrayed, and working with the limitations of the engine was enthralling, and I was very happy to get back into it when updating the game for this portfolio.

Anti-Hero

My latest and most difficult work, Anti-Hero is a Twine game made by myself and drpallid for CTK's 2025 weekend Game Jam, based on the prompt “A Hero's Journey.” While my friend handled art and CSS, my job was to write the branching story and program Sugarcube elements that control the games few mechanics, for instance how much money a player has. After showcasing our work and getting feedback from players, drpallid and I have continued to work on Anti-Hero, with our 1.1 update having released on 2/14/2026 which fixed some of the minor error's my friend and I made in the final, sleep-deprived hours of the Game Jam. This is probably the work I am most proud of, and I cannot wait to create more stories like this!

Honey Ham Questline

The Honey Ham Questline is a multipage document outlining the structure of a single RPG quest. The point of a document like this is to get further into the weeds of the story-mechanic connection so programmers, artists, and other writers all have something to refer to when working on the project. What items are found where, what journal entries update after which encounters, and NPC's players can interact with and more are all explained here, formatted professionally.

SFX Narrative

This short-story of a person going to a ren-faire and buying a cursed object is told only through sound effects mixed in Abode Audition, and shows off my audio design skills. While I may not have a penchant for music, I absolutely love sound effects and take any chance I get to work with them. Finding the right sounds, eq-ing them so to be tinny or boomy or muffled, and then combining them to support or outright tell a story is something I cannot get enough of! I am especially proud of the sound work in this project; there is never a moment where less than two tracks are playing to create the auditory space.

Lord Tille

A high fantasy buddy cop-Esk story staring an aging Lord and a young witch as they travel across the land to clear their names and uncover who really kidnapped King Ionnes, Lord Tille is the reason I am a narrative designer. I never took my writing seriously, but with encouragement from a teacher I started making a book on a whim, and here we are. This story has been re-written and re-edited several times, and with my most recent edits I was finally able to tackle some of this piece's oldest problems. I've known about these issues since I first wrote the text; I knew some sentences refused to flow and certain words felt out of place, but I didn't know how to address them. However, while brushing up my portfolio I finally came up with solutions, and was glad to see this part of Tille's story achieve the quality I always hoped for it.
